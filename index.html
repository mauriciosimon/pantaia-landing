About Me

Designer of Alignment Systems for Decentralized Economies

I've spent most of my entrepreneurial career building network-driven systems and learning where incentive alignment breaks.

At 22, I built a local crowdfunding ecosystem with 300+ investors deploying over $1M into restaurants and events. It successfully activated a community-driven entrepreneurial ecosystem — and taught me that even when traction is strong, systems fail if incentives aren't structurally aligned.

Later, as CEO of a telecom sales organization in Mexico, I designed a decentralized, gamified growth system that scaled from 80 to 180+ employees. It worked because incentives were aligned correctly. It's still operating today.

Now I'm building Majlis.social — using AI agents to create structured mission alignment and quality matching before humans invest time or capital. Launching from Dubai to pioneer agent-to-agent economic infrastructure.

The Vision

"Imagine if every founder or investor landing in Dubai instantly had an AI representative connecting them only to the most aligned opportunities. That's Majlis — an AI-first national networking layer."

The Problem

Founders and family office investors spend enormous time and money networking — traveling to conferences, taking meetings — yet only 1 in 20 conversations is actually relevant. It's not just inefficiency. It's strategic uncertainty: "Am I even in the right rooms?"

New Category

Majlis pioneers a new category: agent-to-agent networking. This doesn't exist in the market. We're not improving networking — we're pre-processing it through intelligence.

Structural Insight

Pakoa proved that when incentives are aligned correctly, decentralized networks scale. Multi-level commission structures, honeycomb community visualization, and real-time performance dashboards created a self-sustaining growth engine.

This experience directly shaped the incentive design philosophy behind Majlis: revenue sharing for community builders who curate high-quality networks.
